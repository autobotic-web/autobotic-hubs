🎤 Presentation Hard to follow; no explanation of what it does; video unclear. Clear demo; explains what the project does in their own words. Confident story: goal → demo → how it works; speaks fluently, anticipates questions.

💻 Coding Code not shown, or clearly copied without understanding. Working code shown; explains the key lines (loop, pins, sleep). Explains the logic fluently; own modifications; clean names; could change it on request.
🐛 Problem-solving Can't name anything that broke or how it was handled. Describes one bug and the fix in their own words. Systematic: what broke, how they diagnosed it (error message, testing), what they'd try first next time.
✨ Creativity & independence Exact copy of the lesson example; heavy help evident. Meaningful variation (own pattern/timing/feature) built mostly solo. Original feature with a clear purpose; explains design choices; built solo.

📌 Video-only assessment guardrails (the video rubric is our ONLY skills instrument — in-class skill scoring is retired):
1. Score what is SHOWN, not how smoothly it's narrated — pins named, code on screen, fail clip. A confident speaker should not drift up on Hardware/Coding; a shy or less fluent speaker should not drift down. Re-records, voiceover and captions are always allowed.
2. Calibrate monthly with anchor videos — keep one clear example of a 2, a 4 and a 6 per skill from real submissions; check your recent scores against them.
3. Review within one week — stale reviews kill posting habits, and the whole skills pillar depends on students posting.
4. No phone ≠ no score — record the student's demo on the centre device during the SHARE step and post it for them.
5. Spot-check against coaching — occasionally ask a live "change X for me now" probe; a scripted video can't survive it.

🐛 Problem-solving: videos must include a ~10 s "fail clip" (something that broke + the fix). No fail clip → score problem-solving from the Mission Log "Broke/Fixed" answers shown on the card, or a 30-second live probe in class ("read me that error — what would you check first?"). Don't penalise hitting MORE bugs — harder bugs usually mean a more ambitious build. Precise help-seeking ("it fails on the second loop, is the sleep wrong?") scores HIGH, not low.

🏆 Best Video of the Month — close voting & award winner

Tallies the month's secret votes, awards the winner(s) a +100 XP bonus row and the 🏆 badge (ties → all winners). Safe to re-run: a student is never awarded twice for the same month. Announce the winner in the Intermediate Group / at Showcase Day!

🏁 IMCC — enter practical scores (90%) & manage quiz attempts

Per mission: practical rubric (90 pts) + quiz (10 pts) = 100. Open a mission and score each rubric component from the official IMCC Rubrics descriptors — the total computes automatically. Quiz points = best quiz % ÷ 10 (3 attempts). ↺ quiz resets a student's attempts (clears their quiz score).
