1 Colour → numbers

The sensor shines a white light and measures how much Red, Green and Blue bounces back. A surface becomes three numbers — and from those, you can name the colour.

🔵 A blue surface reflects lots of blue, little red/green.
⬛ A black surface absorbs almost everything → all readings low.
⬜ A white/silver surface reflects almost everything → all readings high.

🧰 Vocabulary Colour sensor (TCS34725) — reads reflected light as Red/Green/Blue numbers. color_raw — gives (R, G, B, Clear): the four light readings. Threshold — a cut-off you compare against to make a decision. Classify — turn numbers into a label (e.g. "blue") with if rules. Reflect / absorb — light bouncing off vs being soaked up by a surface.
Quick check (say it out loud): in one breath, tell your partner why black gives low RGB and white gives high RGB.
