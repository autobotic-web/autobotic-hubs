📊 How We Score Your Child

Every score on the dashboard comes from a written rubric your teacher follows — nothing is a feeling or a guess. Here's exactly how each number is produced, and what we do to keep scoring fair.

1 · Lessons — skills from build videos

After each lesson your child records a short (10–60 s) video of what they built — including a "fail clip" showing something that went wrong and how they fixed it. The teacher scores the video on five skills, 0–6 each:

SkillWhat earns marks
🎤 PresentationClear demo; explains the project in their own words
🔌 HardwareCorrect setup shown and working; names the components
💻 CodingShows the code and explains the key lines; own modifications
🐛 Problem-solvingNames what broke, how they diagnosed it, what they'd try next time
✨ CreativityTheir own variation or feature, built independently

The Skills Profile on the dashboard averages the most recent reviews, so improvement shows quickly. Quizzes inside each lesson check knowledge separately.

2 · IMCC — the Internal Mini Classroom Competition

Nine robot missions, each scored out of 100: a practical run worth 90 points, judged component by component on a published rubric (e.g. Line Following: Completion 40 + Accuracy 30 + Speed 20), plus a 10-question quiz worth 10 points (best of 3 attempts, marked automatically).

3 · Project — engineering for real

Each student designs and documents their own project (plan, budget, logsheet) and presents it. Scored out of 100:

Functionality 30Explanation 20Design decisions 20 Creativity 10Presentation 10Community engagement 10

Community engagement rewards what your child does — posting their project video on time, replying to comments, and giving helpful feedback on classmates' projects. It is never based on how many likes they receive.

How we keep scoring fair

  • Evidence first: every skill score is anchored to a video or document you can see yourself — ask us, and we'll show you exactly what a score was based on.
  • Anchor calibration: teachers regularly re-check their scoring against reference examples so a "4" means the same thing every month.
  • Shown, not narrated: we score what the video demonstrates, not how confidently a child speaks. Re-recordings, voiceovers and captions are always allowed.
  • Fail clips are rewarded: showing something that broke — and the fix — earns problem-solving marks. We teach that debugging is engineering, not failure.
  • Questions welcome: if any score puzzles you, ask. A score we can't explain to a parent is a score we shouldn't have given.

🔐 Your child's data

What we store: lesson progress and quiz scores, teacher rubric scores and comments, your child's own Mission Log answers, project documents, competition scores, and badges/XP. Build videos live in the members-only class group — visible to enrolled families and teaching staff only, never public unless you separately consent.

  • Your private parent link shows only your own child's progress (first name only, no contact details).
  • You may ask at any time what we hold, ask for corrections, or ask for videos/photos to be removed.
  • Records are kept for the duration of enrollment plus a fixed period stated on your consent form, then deleted.
