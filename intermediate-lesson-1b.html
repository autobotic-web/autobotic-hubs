1 Gather your kit

Tap each item once you've found it:

  • Raspberry Pi 4the little computer itself
  • SD card (32GB)the Pi's "hard drive"
  • SD card readerto flash the OS from your computer
  • USB-C power supply (5V 3A)to power the Pi 4
  • Micro-HDMI to HDMI cableto connect a monitor
  • Keyboard, mouse & monitorto control it
Always insert or remove the SD card with the Pi powered off — doing it while on can corrupt the card.

2 Flash the operating system

The SD card needs the Raspberry Pi OS written onto it. Tick each step:

  • Download Raspberry Pi Imageron your main computer, from raspberrypi.com
  • Insert the SD cardinto your computer's card reader
  • Open Imager → Choose Device & OSpick your Raspberry Pi model, then Raspberry Pi OS (64-bit) — the recommended default for a Pi 4. (For extra pre-installed software: "Raspberry Pi OS (other) → Full".)
  • Choose Storage → your SD carddouble-check it's the right drive — everything on it will be erased!
  • ⚙️ Edit Settings (OS Customisation)In modern Imager, click Edit Settings to preset your username & password, Wi-Fi and language before writing — so your Pi is ready the moment it boots. Note: there's no default "pi / raspberry" login any more, so you must set your own here.
  • Click Writeand wait for it to finish
  • Safely eject the SD cardbefore pulling it out of your computer

3 First boot & setup

Pop the card in, connect everything, and power up. You'll see a rainbow splash, then the desktop. Then tick off the first-time setup:

  • Language & timezonechoose your country and time
  • Create a secure passwordprotects your Pi on the network
  • Connect to Wi-Fipick your network and enter the password
  • Update softwarenow, or later to save time
See a black border around the desktop? That's overscan — uncheck it in the setup wizard or later in Raspberry Pi Configuration and the picture fills the screen.
No picture on the monitor? On a Pi 4, try the other micro-HDMI port, and check the cable is pushed in firmly.

4 Make it yours

Customize the desktop so it feels like home:

  • Change the wallpaper & themePreferences → Appearance Settings
  • Enable single-click to openFile Manager → Edit → Preferences
  • Tidy the taskbarright-click the panel → Panel Settings

5 Why it matters

A few ideas to remember:

Tiny computercredit-card sized, runs Linux

🐛 Bug Hunt — one of these choices would erase the WRONG drive. Click the dangerous line:

Choose OS: Raspberry Pi OS (64-bit)
Choose Storage: my computer's own C: drive
Click Write
Why that line? "Choose Storage" must be your SD card — never your computer's own drive. Write erases everything on whatever you pick, so choosing the C: drive would wipe your whole computer! Always check the drive's name and size before you click Write.
