3 The golden rule

🧩 Fill the blank to complete the rule:

Use AI to ?, then write the answer in your own words.

🐛 Spot the prompt that crosses the line (cheating, not learning) — click it:

"Explain photosynthesis with a simple example"
"Write my whole science essay so I can hand it in"
"Give me 3 practice questions to test myself"
Asking AI to explain or quiz you helps you learn. Asking it to do the work you'll submit as your own is cheating — you learn nothing and it's dishonest. Use AI to understand, then do it yourself.
AI can sound confident but be completely wrong ("hallucinations"). Treat its answers as a draft to check — never the final truth.
✅ Minimum mission success: write ONE strong prompt (Clear + Specific + Context, e.g. "Explain gravity in simple words with a real-life example"), run it in an AI tool yourself, then improve the answer with ONE follow-up question — solo. Show your coach the before/after. Everything past that is bonus.
🤝 Squad Step (partner or group)Now play "Prompt Doctor". As a pair: the navigator picks a weak prompt (like "Explain math") and says how to fix it while the driver types it into the AI — compare the weak vs strong answer, then swap. As a group: everyone fixes the same weak prompt their own way, run them, then vote on whose rewrite got the clearest answer and why.
