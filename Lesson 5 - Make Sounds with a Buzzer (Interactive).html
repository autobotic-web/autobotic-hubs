2 Frequency = pitch

PWM (Pulse Width Modulation) flicks the pin ON and OFF very fast. It has two separate knobs:

🎵 Frequency = how many pulses per second (in hertz, Hz) — this sets the pitch. More pulses per second = higher note. Like a guitar string: tighter and faster wobble = higher note.

🔊 Duty cycle (value, 0.0–1.0) = how much of each pulse is ON — think of it as the "on-ness" that makes the buzzer sound at all. We keep it around 0.5.

To change the note, change the frequency — NOT the duty cycle. This is the classic mix-up: duty cycle makes it louder/fuller, frequency makes it higher or lower.

🐛 Bug Hunt — this buzzer won't make a proper sound. Click the broken line:

buzzer = PWMOutputDevice(17, frequency=440)
buzzer.value = 5
buzzer.frequency = 262
Why that line? value (the duty cycle) must be between 0.0 and 1.0 — 5 is way out of range. Use buzzer.value = 0.5. It's like a dial that only goes 0–1; asking for 5 makes no sense.
