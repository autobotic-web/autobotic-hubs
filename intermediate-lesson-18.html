1 Two motors, four moves

Each wheel is a DC motor with two control lines (forward & backward) through an H-bridge, so it can spin either way. With both wheels you get forward, backward, and turns.

🚗 Forward: both wheels forward.   Backward: both backward.
🔄 Turn left: left wheel backward, right wheel forward. Turn right: the opposite.
🛑 Stop: cut power to both — always end with this!

⚠ Safety: motors make the robot move. Test with the wheels off the ground, use short bursts, and keep a finger on stop() — or it'll drive off the desk.

🧰 Vocabulary DC motor — a motor that spins a wheel; speed and direction are controllable. H-bridge — a circuit that flips current so the motor spins either way. Motor(forward=…, backward=…) — gpiozero's class with the two direction pins. pwm=True — lets you set speed 0.0–1.0, not just on/off. Differential drive — turning by spinning wheels in opposite directions.

🐛 Bug Hunt — the robot zooms off at full speed and errors. Click the broken line:

left_motor.forward(5)
sleep(1)
left_motor.stop()
Why that line? Speed must be 0.0–1.0, and 5 is way out of range. Use forward(0.5) for half speed (or 1.0 for full). Passing a number bigger than 1 is a common slip — the speed isn't a percentage.
